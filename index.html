Compound Information

Mechanism, data & clinical profile.

How retatrutide works, its molecular and stability data, the clinical findings, and the published research behind them.

⚛ Mechanism of Action

One molecule, three receptors

Retatrutide (LY3437943) is a single peptide that activates three metabolic receptors at once. Each pathway contributes a distinct effect — together they drive weight reduction beyond what dual GLP-1/GIP agonists achieve.

GLP-1 receptor

Satiety & glucose

Slows gastric emptying and suppresses appetite through central satiety signalling; stimulates glucose-dependent insulin release and lowers glucagon. The pathway shared with semaglutide.

Appetite · insulin
GIP receptor

Insulin & appetite

Augments glucose-dependent insulin secretion and adds further central appetite suppression, and may support fat metabolism. The second arm of tirzepatide-style dual agonism.

Insulin · fat metabolism
Glucagon receptor

Energy expenditure

Unique among approved and near-approved agents: raises energy expenditure (thermogenesis) and drives hepatic fat oxidation. This third arm is what pushes weight loss past dual-agonist results.

Thermogenesis · fat oxidation

⚠ Side Effects & Tolerability

Monitored signals

  • FatigueMost commonly reported
  • NauseaGLP-1 class; eases with titration
  • Increased heart rateGlucagon-driven; monitor resting HR
  • AnhedoniaReduced reward; monitor mood
  • GI effectsVomiting, diarrhoea; dose-related
  • Skin sensitivityInjection-site or systemic
⛔ Contraindications & Cautions

Where it is not appropriate

  • Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma
  • MEN2 (multiple endocrine neoplasia type 2) syndrome
  • History of pancreatitis
  • Theoretical hepatic risk from the glucagon component
  • Do not combine with other GLP-1 agents (semaglutide, tirzepatide) — additive GI effects and hypoglycaemia risk
